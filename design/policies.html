Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 30, 2026

Information We Collect

Clinical Training and Supervision Academy ("CTA," "we," "us," or "our") collects information you provide when creating an account, enrolling in courses, booking supervision sessions, or contacting us. This may include your name, professional license number, email address, mailing address, phone number, payment information, and documents uploaded for supervision compliance.

We also collect technical data automatically when you use our website and learning platform, including IP address, browser type, device identifiers, pages visited, course progress, and session timestamps. Payment processing is handled by Stripe; we do not store full credit card numbers on our servers.

How We Use Your Information

We use your information to deliver CE courses and supervision services, issue BBS-compliant certificates, manage billing and subscriptions, schedule sessions through our booking system, and communicate important account or policy updates. Course completion data and assessment scores are retained to document compliance with continuing education requirements.

We may use aggregated, de-identified data to improve our platform, analyze usage trends, and develop new offerings. We do not sell your personal information to third parties. Service providers who assist with hosting, email delivery, payment processing, and scheduling access your data only as needed to perform those services under contractual confidentiality obligations.

HIPAA Compliance

CTA provides continuing education and clinical supervision services to licensed and pre-licensed mental health professionals. Our platform is not intended for storage or transmission of protected health information (PHI) belonging to your clients. You agree not to upload client-identifying information, clinical records, or other PHI through course forums, supervision intake forms, or support channels unless explicitly authorized under a separate Business Associate Agreement.

Supervision case summaries submitted through our portal should be de-identified in accordance with applicable privacy standards. If you believe PHI has been submitted in error, contact us immediately at privacy@ctacademy.org so we can assist with remediation. This policy is designed to align with general privacy best practices and does not constitute legal advice regarding your independent HIPAA obligations as a clinician.

Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the right to access, correct, or delete personal information we hold about you, to opt out of certain marketing communications, and to request a portable copy of your account data. California residents may have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), including the right to know what categories of information we collect and to request deletion, subject to legal exceptions.

To exercise these rights, email privacy@ctacademy.org with the subject line "Privacy Request" and allow up to 30 days for a response. We may verify your identity before processing a request. If you are dissatisfied with our response, you may contact the California Attorney General's office or your local data protection authority where applicable.

Refund Policy

Last updated: June 30, 2026

CE Course Refunds

Individual CE course purchases and fixed-price bundles may be refunded in full within seven (7) calendar days of purchase, provided you have not completed more than 25% of the course content or attempted the final examination. Refund requests must be submitted in writing to billing@ctacademy.org and include your order confirmation number.

Once a course has been substantially accessed or a certificate has been issued, the purchase is considered fulfilled and is not eligible for refund. Partial refunds are not offered for unused portions of a course. Approved refunds are processed to the original payment method within 5–10 business days.

Supervision Session Cancellations

Supervision sessions scheduled through our platform may be canceled or rescheduled at no charge when notice is provided at least 24 hours before the scheduled start time. Cancellations made with less than 24 hours' notice, or failure to attend a confirmed session ("no-show"), may result in forfeiture of the session fee or deduction from your prepaid session balance, at the discretion of your assigned supervisor.

If CTA or your supervisor cancels a session, you will receive a full credit toward a rescheduled appointment or a refund of any prepaid amount associated with that session. Emergency cancellations due to illness or unforeseen circumstances may be reviewed on a case-by-case basis by contacting support@ctacademy.org.

Subscription Cancellations

Monthly subscription plans, including the Supervision + CE Hybrid membership, may be canceled at any time through your account dashboard. Cancellation stops future billing cycles; access continues through the end of the current paid period. Refunds are not issued for partial months or unused time within an active billing cycle unless required by law or explicitly stated in a promotional offer.

Annual All-Access Pass subscriptions may be refunded within fourteen (14) days of purchase if less than 25% of included course content has been accessed. After this window, annual plans are non-refundable but may be canceled before renewal to prevent future charges. All subscription refund determinations are final after review by our billing team.

Grievance Policy

Last updated: June 30, 2026

How to File a Grievance

If you have a complaint regarding course content, instructor conduct, supervision services, accessibility, billing, or any aspect of your experience with CTA, we encourage you to submit a formal grievance in writing. Send your complaint to grievances@ctacademy.org or mail it to Clinical Training and Supervision Academy, Attn: Grievance Officer, California, USA.

Your submission should include your full name, contact information, license type and number (if applicable), a clear description of the issue, dates of relevant interactions, and any supporting documentation. Anonymous grievances may be reviewed at our discretion but may limit our ability to investigate fully or provide a direct response.

Review Process

All grievances are acknowledged within three (3) business days of receipt. A designated Grievance Officer will review the complaint, gather relevant records, and may contact you or involved parties for additional information. We aim to issue a written resolution within thirty (30) days; complex matters may require an extension, in which case you will be notified of the revised timeline.

Resolutions may include corrective action, policy clarification, refunds or credits where warranted, changes to course or supervision assignments, or a determination that the complaint is unfounded. If you disagree with the outcome, you may request a secondary review by emailing grievances@ctacademy.org within fourteen (14) days of receiving our decision.

BBS Reporting Information

Clinical Training and Supervision Academy is an approved continuing education provider under the California Board of Behavioral Sciences. If your grievance relates to CE provider conduct and remains unresolved after completing our internal process, you have the right to file a complaint directly with the BBS.

Contact the Board of Behavioral Sciences at bbs.ca.gov or by mail at the address published on the BBS website. Include our provider name and number, course title, and a summary of your concern. CTA cooperates fully with BBS inquiries and maintains records of grievances and resolutions as required by applicable regulations. Nothing in this policy prevents you from pursuing remedies available under state or federal law.
